Health Insurance Policy - Sample Coverage Document

Section 1: Hospitalization Benefits
The insured is entitled to coverage for hospitalization expenses up to Rs. 5,00,000 per policy year. 
This includes room rent up to Rs. 5,000 per day, ICU charges up to Rs. 10,000 per day, and surgeon fees. 
Pre-hospitalization expenses are covered for 30 days prior to admission, and post-hospitalization 
expenses are covered for 60 days after discharge.

Section 2: Pre-Existing Diseases
Pre-existing diseases are covered after a waiting period of 4 years from the policy inception date. 
Conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, and asthma are treated as pre-existing if diagnosed 
before purchasing the policy. The policyholder must disclose all pre-existing conditions at the time of application.

Section 3: Exclusions
The policy does not cover cosmetic surgeries, dental treatments (unless due to accident), 
maternity expenses (covered under a separate add-on rider), injuries from self-harm, 
treatment outside India, and experimental treatments not approved by medical authorities.

Section 4: Cashless Hospitalization
Cashless treatment is available at 5,000+ network hospitals across India. To avail cashless benefits,
the insured must inform the insurance company within 24 hours of emergency hospitalization, 
or 48 hours before planned hospitalization. The Third Party Administrator (TPA) must approve the cashless request.

Section 5: Claim Process
For reimbursement claims, the insured must submit all original bills, discharge summary, 
doctor prescriptions, and investigation reports within 30 days of discharge. 
Claims are processed within 15 working days of receiving complete documentation. 
Any disputes should be escalated to the grievance cell within 30 days of claim rejection.

Section 6: Critical Illness Rider
An optional Critical Illness rider covers 32 critical conditions including cancer, heart attack, 
kidney failure, stroke, and organ transplants. The lump sum benefit of Rs. 10,00,000 is paid 
on first diagnosis of any covered condition. There is a 90-day survival clause from the date of diagnosis.

Section 7: No Claim Bonus
For every claim-free year, the sum insured increases by 10% as No Claim Bonus (NCB), 
up to a maximum of 50% of the original sum insured. If a claim is made, the NCB reduces 
by 10% for each claim year but does not go below the original sum insured.

Section 8: Premium and Renewal
The premium is calculated based on age, sum insured, medical history, and add-on riders selected. 
The policy must be renewed before the expiry date to maintain continuity benefits. 
A grace period of 30 days is allowed for renewal. Lifelong renewability is guaranteed 
irrespective of claims or health deterioration.

Motor Insurance Policy - Third Party and Comprehensive Coverage

Section 1: Third Party Liability
Third party liability insurance is mandatory under the Motor Vehicles Act. It covers bodily injury, 
death, or property damage caused to a third party due to an accident involving the insured vehicle. 
There is no upper limit on third party bodily injury claims. Property damage to third party 
is covered up to Rs. 7.5 lakh per accident.

Section 2: Own Damage Coverage
Comprehensive motor insurance covers own damage to the insured vehicle due to accidents, 
theft, fire, natural calamities (flood, earthquake, cyclone), riots, and malicious acts. 
The Insured Declared Value (IDV) represents the maximum amount payable in case of total loss or theft.

Section 3: Add-On Covers
Optional add-on covers include Zero Depreciation (full claim without depreciation deduction), 
Engine Protection (covers hydrostatic lock and oil leakage), Return to Invoice (claim up to original 
purchase price in case of total loss), Roadside Assistance (24x7 towing and emergency help), 
and Personal Accident Cover for passengers.

Section 4: Claim Intimation
Any claim must be intimated to the insurer within 24 hours of the accident or incident. 
For theft claims, an FIR must be filed immediately and submitted with the claim. 
The vehicle should not be repaired before the survey, except for minor repairs to prevent 
further damage, which must be pre-approved by the insurer.
